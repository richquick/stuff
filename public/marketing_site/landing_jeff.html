How to implement content strategy on agency projects with Jeff Eaton

If you've had enough of clients who don't understand the importance of thinking content-first and aren't willing to hire you to help them, then this session is for you.

Jeff Eaton will walk you through two case-studies and explain how he goes about successfully implementing content strategy services at his agency.

Whether you are already offering content strategy services, or are thinking about it, Jeff will shed light on approach and process. You'll learn:

  • about the process, decisions and challenges your team will face
  • how content strategy meshes within various agency roles
  • how you can develop a playbook for your projects
  • what deliverables you should focus on, and how to use them
  • how to avoid joining the bucket brigade (hang in there)
  • to use content strategy to make your projects more successful
  • and, how to go about billing for this work

We'd recommend bringing your entire project team along to this webinar if you are feeling any resistance to internal process changes.

Who is Jeff?

Glad you asked. Jeff brings more than two decades of experience in publishing, enterprise infrastructure, and web development. He's built e-commerce sites for florists, enterprise web systems for multinational corporations, and supply-chain automation tools for billion-dollar industries. At Lullabot, he's designed and implemented large-scale web platforms for clients including Sony/BMG Music, Fast Company and Inc. Magazine, World Wrestling Entertainment, Verizon Wireless, Harvard University, and more.

When he's not writing or speaking about multichannel publishing, structured content, and the business value of streamlined editorial workflow, Jeff hosts the Insert Content Here content strategy podcast. In the Drupal world, he's best known as a co-author of O'Reilly Media's Using Drupal; author of the popular Voting API, EVA, and Token projects as well as dozens of other plugin modules; the primary developer of Drupal's core TokenAPI and a co-developer of FormAPI; and member of the content advisory committee for Drupal.org.

All-in-all, a well rounded chap.
